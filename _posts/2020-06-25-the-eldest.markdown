I am truly blessed to have two amazing kids. They are kind, sweet, and everything that I could ever dream of.

This particular entry is for me to look back in a few year's time at some of the highlights of what Toby has said and done in the past. 


When Toby was 3, we got him his first Lego, it was a Mixel pack. He was only limited in his lack of finger strength to do the whole set by himself, we must've put together and took the set apart a dozen times in a day.

When Toby was 3 and a half, we got him am Osmo Tangram game for Christmas. I think it was the first toy that captivated him other than Lego. He was at his station for hours solving easy to hard clues. Quite the sight to see.

When Toby was 4 he asked if I was allergic to milk, I told him yes. Which prompted him to ask "How? Isn't yogurt made from milk? But I've seen you eat yogurt", I explained that some special yogurts had labels that made it ok for me to have it. Which he then extrapolated back to ask if there were some Milks with these labels, and then explored into cheeses and other milk products.

By 4 and a half, having understood additions and subtractions, when I explained multiplications, he asked if there was a reverse multiplications. He then understood the concept of divisions right away.

When Toby was 5 he asked why some animals were so similar to each other when others weren't. I explained about Taxonomy, and then we spent the whole week classifying different life forms. He would beg me each night, to classify just one more.
